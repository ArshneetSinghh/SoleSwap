The environmental impact of sneaker production

The World Footwear Yearbook estimates that approximately 24 billion pairs of shoes are manufactured yearly (mostly in conditions that are exploitative), with sneakers making up the greatest portion.

Why is it so difficult to cut down on plastic use? The durability factor is crucial for sneakers since they must withstand significantly more wear and tear than a typical pair of shoes. Unfortunately, synthetic materials perform better than organic ones when it comes to durability. Shoes are now better, lighter, quicker, more comfortable, and more widely available thanks to plastic. But another issue is that the majority of shoes are produced from a mix of several polymers that are sewn and bonded in extremely intricate ways, making them very difficult to recycle.

What do brands do to address their environmental impact?

Tansy Hoskins, author of the book Foot Work: What Your Shoes are Doing to the World, claims that the footwear business is at least 10 years behind the rest of the fashion sector in terms of human rights and environmental norms.
