Question: What is the clear view sole made from?
Answer: Thermoplasticized rubber is used to create our clear view sole (TPR). TPR is a fantastic option because it is produced without PVC and does not require the use of BPA. On teethers and the spouts of Sippy cups, TPR is typically utilised in regions that require a lot of "rubber." Since we are aware that everything might end up in a child's mouth, what better material could we use to manufacture our soles?

Question: Are Sole Swap shoes waterproof?
Answer: Consisting of a proprietary set of U-shaped rings that connect the uppers to the soles to form a water-resistant barrier.

Question: Are you certain that walking won't cause the sole to come off?
Answer: The top of the shoes is fastened with a number of rings. The outsole of the shoe has grooves that the rings slide into. So it won't come loose from the sole.
